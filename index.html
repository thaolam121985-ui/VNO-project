Living traditions

A culture 4,000 years in the making

🍜

Food as identity

From Phở in the North to Bánh Mì in the South, Vietnamese cuisine varies dramatically by region — each dish a reflection of local landscape and history.

🎋

Tết & festivals

The Lunar New Year (Tết) is the most important celebration, but Vietnam's calendar is packed with harvest festivals, lantern days, and temple fairs.

🏺

Craft traditions

Silk weaving in Vạn Phúc, lacquerware in Hà Nội, and pottery in Bát Tràng — hundreds of craft villages still practice ancient techniques.

🙏

Spiritual life

A blend of Buddhism, Confucianism, and Taoism shapes daily life — seen in pagoda incense, ancestor altars, and morning market offerings.

Before you go

Essential travel tips

01

Best time to visit

November–April for the South and Central Coast. North Vietnam is best April–June to avoid summer rains.

02

Visa requirements

Many nationalities can get an e-visa valid for 90 days online via the official Vietnam Immigration portal.

03

Getting around

Grab app for cities, overnight sleeper trains between major stops, and budget flights for longer distances.

04

Currency & payment

Vietnamese Đồng (VND). Street food and markets are cash-only; cities increasingly accept cards.

05

Language basics

Vietnamese is tonal — locals appreciate even a simple "xin chào" (hello) or "cảm ơn" (thank you).

06

Temple etiquette

Cover shoulders and knees at pagodas, remove shoes when entering, and ask before photographing ceremonies.
